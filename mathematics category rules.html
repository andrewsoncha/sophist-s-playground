Mathematics Category Rules

About

In the Mathematics category, an explicitly incorrect mathematical statement will be announced for the participants to prove. Participants are free to utilize mathematical theorems, definitions of mathematical expressions, and set up variables and functions. While it doesn’t matter whether the utilization of the mathematical techniques was appropriate or correct, the mathematical technique utilized by the participants must be mathematically legitimate unless it is proven in an earlier part of the proof. For example, participants cannot utilize the argument “(a+b)(c+d)=ac+bd” without proving it through using other correct mathematical techniques since the argument itself is incorrect.

Winner Selection

The submitted proofs will be ranked on two categories: Creativity and apparent legitimacy. Creativity refers to how creatively the proof utilized mathematical theorems and expressions. Apparent legitimacy refers to the degree the proof looks mathematically true and how well the leap in logic is hidden from the viewer. Apparent legitimacy has nothing to do with whether the proof is actually logically true since all submitted proofs are false, and only has to do if it “looks true” at a first glance.

Submission Form

Participants are to submit their proofs in the form of a word document or a jpg image file of the picture of the proof. We highly recommend participants to read some of the example proofs to get a sense of how to write and format their proofs.
